# Fizzmind — Programs & Curriculum

## Overview

Every Fizzmind camper chooses a **primary track** — their main focus area for project time each morning. But every camper also participates in the shared afternoon adventures and evening connection activities, regardless of track.

The tracks are NOT classes. There are no textbooks, no exams, no grades. Each track is a guided journey of building, creating, and discovering — with real tools, real output, and real fun.

## Track 1: STEM Explorer

**Color:** Blue (#3B82F6)
**For:** Builders, coders, scientists, engineers, robot obsessives

### Philosophy
Real tools, real projects, real output. No "intro to coding" worksheets. From day one, kids are building things that work. We meet them where they are — beginners get scaffolded up, advanced kids go deep.

### Week-by-Week Curriculum

#### Week 1: Foundation & Discovery
**Days 1-2: What Do You Want to Build?**
- Icebreaker: "Show me the coolest thing on your phone/laptop" — immediate tech conversation
- Survey of interests: robotics, AI, web, games, hardware, data
- Form project teams (3-4 per team) based on shared interests
- Tool setup: Python, VS Code, Arduino IDE, Raspberry Pi kits distributed
- Mini-challenge: Build something that works in 2 hours (any tool, any idea)

**Days 3-5: Core Skills Sprint**
- Python fundamentals (for beginners) / Advanced Python (for experienced)
- Hardware basics: wiring, sensors, motors, LEDs (hands-on, not theory)
- AI introduction: What is a model? Train a simple image classifier
- Each team begins their main project — counselor-guided planning session
- End of week: 2-minute project pitch to the cohort ("here's what we're building and why")

#### Week 2: Build Sprint
**Days 6-10: Deep Project Work**
- Full mornings devoted to project building
- Daily standup: each team shares progress and blockers (5 min)
- Skills workshops (optional, 30 min): Git basics, debugging strategies, 3D modeling, API calls, sensor integration
- Cross-team collaboration: teams can request help from other teams
- Mid-week checkpoint: demo current progress, get peer feedback
- Friday: Project polish and prep for final presentation

#### Week 3 (3-week camps only): Advanced & Presentation
**Days 11-15: Push the Limits**
- Advanced workshops: machine learning with real datasets, computer vision, autonomous systems
- Teams enhance projects with stretch goals
- Individual passion projects (for those who want to explore solo)
- Dress rehearsal presentations
- Final Demo Day: present to the full cohort + invited guests (parents via video)
- Each team gets a recorded video of their demo

### Example Projects (past camps)
- Autonomous line-following robot with obstacle avoidance
- AI-powered plant health monitor (camera + trained model)
- Weather station dashboard with real-time sensor data
- Multiplayer game built in Python with networking
- Drone that follows a colored object using computer vision
- Smart door lock with facial recognition (Raspberry Pi)

### Tools & Equipment Provided
- Raspberry Pi 4 kits (one per team)
- Arduino Uno kits with sensors, motors, LEDs
- 3D printer access (shared)
- Drone kits (supervised use)
- Laptops with Python, VS Code, Jupyter, TensorFlow Lite pre-installed
- Soldering stations (supervised, 12+ only)
- Electronics components library

## Track 2: Creative Arts

**Color:** Pink (#EC4899)
**For:** Artists, filmmakers, musicians, designers, storytellers, performers

### Philosophy
Create something real and share it with the world. Not "art class" — guerrilla filmmaking, real music production, street art on actual walls, theater that makes people laugh and cry. Every kid leaves with a portfolio of work they're genuinely proud of.

### Week-by-Week Curriculum

#### Week 1: Discover Your Medium
**Days 1-2: Creative Explosion**
- Icebreaker: 60-second talent showcase — anything goes
- Rapid rotation: 30 min each of filmmaking, music, visual art, digital design, writing, improv
- Find your thing: pick primary medium + secondary interest
- Form creative crews (2-4 per crew)

**Days 3-5: Foundation Sprint**
- **Film crew:** Camera basics, framing, editing (DaVinci Resolve), storyboarding
- **Music crew:** GarageBand/Ableton basics, beat making, recording vocals
- **Visual arts crew:** Sketching, acrylic painting, digital illustration (Procreate/tablet)
- **Design crew:** Canva, Figma basics, typography, color theory
- **Writing crew:** Flash fiction, screenwriting, spoken word, journaling
- Mini-project: Create something in your medium in 3 hours, share at evening showcase

#### Week 2: Major Project
**Days 6-10: Create Something Ambitious**
- **48-Hour Film Challenge** (film crews): write, shoot, and edit a short film in 48 hours
- **Album Track** (music crews): produce one finished song — lyrics, beat, recording, mixing
- **Mural Project** (visual arts): large-scale collaborative artwork on a real wall
- **Magazine** (design + writing): design and publish a physical camp magazine
- **Improv Show** (performers): write and rehearse a 15-minute show
- Daily feedback sessions: crews share work-in-progress, get constructive input
- Friday: Camp Creative Festival — all work exhibited/performed

#### Week 3 (3-week camps only): Master & Exhibit
- Individual passion project — create one piece you're deeply proud of
- Advanced workshops: animation, music theory, portrait drawing, documentary filmmaking
- Curate a personal portfolio (digital)
- Final Exhibition Night: gallery + screening + performance for the full camp

### Tools & Equipment Provided
- DSLR cameras + tripods (shared, supervised)
- Editing laptops with DaVinci Resolve, Audacity, Figma
- Drawing tablets (Wacom) for digital illustration
- Music equipment: MIDI keyboards, microphones, headphones, audio interface
- Art supplies: acrylics, canvas, brushes, spray paint (supervised)
- Printing equipment for magazines/zines

## Track 3: Leadership & Entrepreneurship

**Color:** Amber (#F59E0B)
**For:** Future founders, debaters, organizers, social impact leaders, big-picture thinkers

### Philosophy
Lead by doing, not by reading about leadership. Kids build a real mini-business, run a real event, pitch to real judges. They learn public speaking by speaking publicly, negotiation by negotiating, and leadership by leading.

### Week-by-Week Curriculum

#### Week 1: Lead Yourself
**Days 1-2: Who Are You?**
- Strengths assessment: what's your superpower?
- Communication styles workshop
- Public speaking crash course: everyone gives a 2-minute talk on Day 1 and Day 5 — watch the transformation
- Debate fundamentals: how to argue respectfully

**Days 3-5: Lead Others**
- Team formation: each team of 4-5 gets a real challenge
- Challenge examples: plan and run a camp event, solve a local community problem, create a fundraising campaign
- Project management basics: timelines, roles, accountability
- Negotiation game: multi-team resource trading simulation
- Financial literacy: budgets, pricing, revenue basics

#### Week 2: Build a Business
**Days 6-10: Startup Sprint**
- Each team builds a mini-business from scratch:
  - Day 6: Ideation — what problem are you solving?
  - Day 7: Market research (talk to real people)
  - Day 8: Build your MVP (product, service, or prototype)
  - Day 9: Marketing and branding
  - Day 10: **Pitch Day** — present to a panel of real judges (local entrepreneurs, counselors)
- Winning team gets to execute their idea during camp (with support)

#### Week 3 (3-week camps only): Impact & Legacy
- Social impact project: identify a real issue in the local community, design and begin a solution
- Advanced debate tournament
- Personal brand workshop: LinkedIn-for-kids (age-appropriate online presence)
- TEDx-style final talks: each kid gives a 5-minute talk on something they care about

### Skills Developed
- Public speaking and presentation
- Team leadership and delegation
- Financial literacy and budgeting
- Negotiation and persuasion
- Project management
- Social entrepreneurship
- Critical thinking and debate

## Track 4: Sports & Adventure

**Color:** Green (#10B981)
**For:** Athletic kids, outdoor enthusiasts, adrenaline seekers, team players

### Philosophy
Physical challenge builds mental strength. Sports aren't just about winning — they're about learning to push through discomfort, trust your teammates, and discover what your body can do. Every kid finds a sport they love, even the ones who think they "aren't sporty."

### Week-by-Week Curriculum

#### Week 1: Discover Your Sport
**Days 1-2: Try Everything**
- Sport rotation: soccer, basketball, volleyball, swimming, rock climbing, martial arts basics
- Fitness assessment (fun, not clinical): what's your strength?
- Team formation based on interests
- Outdoor skills basics: map reading, compass navigation, knot tying

**Days 3-5: Skill Building**
- Primary sport deep-dive (chosen by team)
- Swimming proficiency sessions
- Rock climbing progression (indoor wall → outdoor if available)
- Yoga and mindfulness (daily, 20 min)
- Introduction to parkour (urban movement, safe environment)
- Capture the flag at EPIC scale (whole camp)

#### Week 2: Challenge & Competition
**Days 6-10: Push Your Limits**
- Multi-day hiking expedition (overnight camping, 2 days)
- Camp Olympics: multi-sport tournament between teams
- Obstacle course challenge (built by the kids themselves!)
- Water sports day: kayaking, paddle boarding, swimming races
- Adventure challenge: orienteering, survival skills, team problem-solving
- Sports science workshop: how does your body work? Nutrition, recovery, biomechanics

#### Week 3 (3-week camps only): Mastery & Leadership
- Advanced climbing (outdoor, with certified guides)
- Kids design and run their own mini sports tournament for the camp
- Sports coaching workshop: learn to teach others
- Extended expedition: 3-day trek with camping
- Final camp-wide sports day: organized entirely by this track's kids

### Safety
- All activities supervised by qualified sports instructors
- Equipment provided (helmets, harnesses, life jackets)
- Medical staff on call
- Activities graded by ability — no one is forced beyond their comfort zone
- Hydration and sun protection protocols at all locations

## Track 5: Global Citizenship

**Color:** Purple (#8B5CF6)
**For:** Culture enthusiasts, language lovers, future diplomats, environmentally conscious kids

### Philosophy
The world is better when we understand each other. This track turns cultural curiosity into real connection. Kids don't just learn ABOUT other cultures — they learn FROM other kids who live them.

### Week-by-Week Curriculum

#### Week 1: Your World & Theirs
**Days 1-2: Who's in the Room?**
- Cultural mapping: where is everyone from? What languages do we speak?
- Language exchange: teach 5 words in your language to the group
- Cultural show-and-tell: bring something from home (object, recipe, song, game)
- World map project: physical map on the wall, marked with everyone's connections

**Days 3-5: Going Deeper**
- Language immersion games: survival phrases in 3 languages
- Cooking battle: teams make dishes from different cultures (guided by kids who know them)
- Sustainability workshop: what does climate change look like in YOUR country?
- Model UN prep: choose a country, research its position on a global issue
- Cultural etiquette game: learn greetings, table manners, and customs from around the world

#### Week 2: Act & Impact
**Days 6-10: Make It Real**
- **Model UN simulation** (2 days): full formal debate on a real global issue
- Community service project: partner with a local organization for a half-day volunteer experience
- Sustainability build: design and create something that solves an environmental problem (physical prototype)
- International film screening + discussion: watch a film from a culture no one in the group represents
- Cultural festival: each kid hosts a "booth" representing their culture — food, music, crafts, stories
- Pen pal setup: connect with a kid at another Fizzmind location for ongoing correspondence

#### Week 3 (3-week camps only): Ambassador
- Deep language immersion: choose one language, reach conversational basics in a week
- Extended community service project
- Write and present a "My Country" essay — share your home through your eyes
- Global solutions pitch: present a solution to a real-world problem to the full camp
- Certificate of Global Citizenship

### Languages Available for Immersion
Varies by location:
- California/New York: Spanish, Mandarin
- Dubai: Arabic, Hindi
- KL: Malay, Mandarin
- Singapore: Mandarin, Malay, Tamil
- London: French, Spanish
- Paris: French (primary), Arabic
- Berlin: German, Turkish
- Barcelona: Spanish, Catalan

## Cross-Track Activities

These happen for ALL campers, regardless of track:

### Daily
- Morning Circle (whole cohort)
- Afternoon Adventure (mixed-track teams)
- Evening Connection

### Weekly
- Cross-Track Collaboration Day: tracks work together on a shared challenge
- Talent Show Night: anyone can perform anything
- Culture Night: rotating cultural themes
- Camp Council: kids propose and vote on activities

### End of Camp
- Demo Day / Exhibition / Performance (track-specific)
- Awards ceremony (fun awards, not competitive rankings):
  - "The Spark" — most curious camper
  - "The Connector" — built the most bridges between people
  - "The Builder" — most ambitious project
  - "The Heart" — kindest, most supportive
  - "The Wildcard" — surprised everyone
- Farewell campfire with memory sharing

## Assessment & Progress

### During Camp
- No grades, no scores, no rankings
- Counselors maintain observation journals for each camper
- Daily check-ins between counselor and camper (informal, 2-3 min)
- Peer feedback sessions during project work

### End of Camp Report (sent to parents)
- Personal narrative about the camper's experience (written by lead counselor)
- Skills developed (specific to their track)
- Social growth observations
- Project portfolio (photos, videos, links to digital work)
- Recommendations for continued learning at home
- Counselor's personal note to the camper
